# Spacecraft In-Game Shipbuilding Specification

This document details the core shipbuilding and flight systems as they function in the actual game. It acts as a reference for developing and verifying the Spacecraft Shipbuilder web application.

---

## 1. Grid & Coordinate System

* **Integer-Based Grid**: All block anchors, dimensions, and positions are defined in integer grid units.
* **Anchor Point**: A block's position is defined by its minimum corner $[X_{min}, Y_{min}, Z_{min}]$, which simplifies collision detection and snapping.
* **Boundary Rules**: There are no hard boundary limits on the grid size, but all block placements are calculated relative to the ship's cockpit/core.
* **Ground Plane**: The build grid has a floor boundary at $Y = 0$. Blocks cannot be placed below this level.

---

## 2. Adjacency & Connectivity Rules

* **Contiguous Structure**: A ship must be a single, physically connected structure. Floating or disconnected blocks are not allowed.
* **Connection Type**: Blocks must connect via **face-to-face contact** with adjacent blocks. Edge-to-edge or corner-to-corner contacts do not count as structural connections.
* **Core Dependency**: Every block must connect directly or indirectly (through other connected blocks) back to the cockpit/core block. If a block is disconnected, it will fall off or be disallowed.

---

## 3. Orientation & Flip System

In-game block orientation differs from free 3D rotation:
* **Flipping over Rotation**: There is no free 90-degree or 270-degree yaw/pitch/roll rotation. Blocks have a default predefined orientation and are adjusted via three independent binary flips:
  * **Flip X**: Mirrors the block along the East-West axis.
  * **Flip Y**: Mirrors the block along the Up-Down axis (toggles upside down).
  * **Flip Z**: Mirrors the block along the North-South axis.
* **Grid Bounds Impact**: Because there is no 90-degree rotation, a block's dimensions along the X, Y, and Z axes are always equal to its default dimensions. Mirroring/flipping only affects visual representation and internal/docking details; it does not change the grid occupancy bounds.

---

## 4. System Support (SP) & Efficiency

* **SP Contributors**: Cockpits, structural blocks (Steel, Titanium), and wings add to the ship's total **System Support (SP)**.
* **SP Consumers**: Components like thrusters, mining lasers, storage, weapons, etc. consume SP as **System Requirements**.
* **Over-allocation & Efficiency**:
  * The ship remains functional if the total consumed SP exceeds the total provided SP.
  * However, going over the limit causes the ship's efficiency to drop.
  * **Efficiency Calculation**:
    $$\text{Efficiency} = \min\left(1.0, \frac{\text{Total SP Provided}}{\text{Total SP Consumed}}\right)$$
  * *Example*: If components consume 2260 SP but the ship only provides 2100 SP, the ship runs at $92\%$ efficiency.

---

## 5. Power System (Energy Balance)

* **Global Power Balance**: Power is managed globally across the ship.
* **Power Generators**: Power is generated by specific blocks, including certain cockpits, solar panels, batteries (recharge/discharge), and fuel generators.
* **Power Consumers**: Active components (thrusters, lasers, weapons) consume power.
* **Battery Requirement**: Batteries are mandatory for components to draw power.
* **Depletion/Out-of-Power Effect**:
  * If power is completely depleted (consumption exceeds generation and battery reserves are empty), active components cannot be used.
  * The ship can still fly, but at a **severely reduced speed (~15% of normal)**.

---

## 6. Heat & Thermal System

* **Global Heat Pool**: Heat is simulated as a global ship-wide metric rather than being localized.
* **Heat Capacity**: Sum of the heat capacities of all blocks. It represents the total thermal energy required to raise the ship's temperature and how much heat it can absorb before overheating.
* **Heat Conductivity & Interface**:
  * A material's heat conductivity determines how fast heat travels through it.
  * Highly conductive materials increase the ship's global **heat interface**, causing it to adjust temperature more rapidly to external environmental conditions.
* **Heat Generation**: Active components (especially thrusters) generate heat.
* **Heat Dissipation**: Cockpits and radiators dissipate heat globally.
* **Overheating**: If global heat generation continuously exceeds global heat dissipation, the ship heats up globally and will overheat, causing systems to fail or take damage.

---

## 7. Weight, Frame & Structural Integrity

* **Weight**: Total mass of the ship (sum of all block weights). Weight increases thrust requirements and affects agility.
* **Frame**: The structural capacity of the ship to not break under its own weight. Provided by structural blocks and cockpits.
* **Structural Integrity**: A relationship between Total Frame and Total Weight. High weight requires sufficient Frame points to maintain structural integrity.
* **Hull Strength**: The total health pool of the ship (sum of all block hull values). If Hull HP falls to 0, the ship is disabled ("out of order") and requires towing.

---

## 8. Thrusters & Flight Dynamics

* **Force vs. Thrust**:
  * **Force**: Represents the engine load capacity/power limit.
  * **Thrust**: The actual forward linear push force measured in kilonewtons (kN).
* **Steering Strength**: Determines the ship's rotational agility.
* **Boost Mechanics**:
  * Boosting adds its values to the normal operating values:
    * $\text{Total Thrust} = \text{Normal Thrust} + \text{Boost Thrust}$
    * $\text{Total Power Consumption} = \text{Normal Power} + \text{Boost Power}$
    * $\text{Total Heat Generation} = \text{Normal Heat} + \text{Boost Heat}$
* **Agility Center-of-Mass (CoM)**: In the basic flight calculation, thruster placement location relative to the Center of Mass does not dynamically scale the steering or thrust direction.

---

## 9. Block Shapes & Aesthetics

* **Cosmetic Hull Shapes**: Hull shapes (Full Block, Slope, Slope Flat) are **purely cosmetic**.
* **Dimensions & Stats**: Selecting a Slope or Slope Flat does not reduce the block's physical grid collision bounds, weight, hull strength, or resource costs. They are treated identical to a Full Block by the physics and calculation engines.

---

## 10. Gap Analysis: Current App vs. In-Game Mechanics

Below is a comparison of how the current web application matches up with the true in-game behavior, highlighting what needs to be added or modified in the app:

| Mechanic | In-Game Behavior | Current App Implementation | Status/Gap |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Grid Boundaries** | Infinite relative to core, minimum floor plane at $Y = 0$. | Implemented floor check at $Y = 0$. | Matches |
| **Adjacency / Connection** | Contiguous structure; face-to-face connection to cockpit required. | No connection check; blocks can float disconnected. | **Gap: Needs connectivity validation.** |
| **Orientation** | Predefined direction + 3 binary flips (X, Y, Z). No 90-degree yaw/pitch/roll. | Supports 90-degree rotation axes via `rotateBlock` and Euler angles. | **Gap: Alignment with binary flips constraint.** |
| **SP & Efficiency** | Summed SP; efficiency drops if consumed > provided. | Simple sums of stats; no efficiency formula. | **Gap: Needs efficiency calculation & display.** |
| **Power System** | Global power balance; batteries required; speed drops to 15% if depleted. | Stats summed; no power balance or battery requirements modeled. | **Gap: Needs power balance and speed-reduction logic.** |
| **Heat / Thermal** | Global heat pool; capacity, dissipation, environment-linked interface. | Simple stats summing; no heat pool simulation. | **Gap: Needs heat pool balance calculations.** |
| **Frame / Integrity** | Frame vs. Weight determines structural integrity. | Stats summed; no integrity ratio displayed. | **Gap: Needs structural integrity ratio display.** |
| **Thruster Boost** | Boost values are added to normal values. | Standard stats summed; no separate Boost mode calculation. | **Gap: Needs Boost toggle and additive math.** |
| **Block Shapes** | Cosmetic; bounds and stats remain full. | Visual meshes change; bounds are rotated via Euler math. | **Gap: Align collision bounds for shape variants.** |
